Chief Complaint

Excessive mucus production, chronic throat clearing, and coughing — occurring after nearly every meal and waking me at approximately 3:30 AM most nights with reflux, coughing, and mucus.

This has been a lifelong pattern that has significantly escalated over the past ~2 years into a nightly occurrence affecting sleep (averaging 3–4 hours).

Suspected Diagnosis

Laryngopharyngeal Reflux (LPR) — "silent reflux" where stomach contents reach the throat and larynx, triggering mucus production as a protective response.

Symptoms are consistent with LPR rather than classic GERD: no significant heartburn, but heavy throat/airway involvement.

Key Context — Thyroid Connection
Recently diagnosed with autoimmune thyroid condition (elevated TPO antibodies). Currently on levothyroxine, approximately 6–8 weeks into treatment. This is likely the upstream driver of the escalation:
  • Reduced gastric motility — food sits longer, more reflux opportunity
  • Reduced esophageal motility — acid clears more slowly when it does reflux
  • Elevated histamine reactivity — mucous membranes overreact to acid exposure
  • Disrupted sleep architecture — less overnight tissue repair

The escalation from occasional flare-ups to nightly episodes correlates with the timeline of thyroid dysfunction (~2 years). Expect improvement as thyroid levels optimize.

Current Management

Three-mechanism approach (started April 2026):

  • PPI: Omeprazole 20mg — 30 min before dinner
  • H2 blocker: Famotidine 20mg — bedtime
  • H1 antihistamine: Cetirizine 10mg — daily

Also using: DAO enzyme with meals, Gaviscon as needed

Sleep position: head elevated via adjustable bed

Other Relevant History
  • Known histamine sensitivity with identified food triggers
  • Previous visit with this ENT for travel antibiotics
  • Eustachian tube dysfunction / tinnitus (also likely thyroid-related)
  • Traveling frequently — symptoms worsen with disrupted routines
What I'd Like From This Visit

1. Laryngoscopy — scope the throat and vocal cords to assess for visible signs of chronic acid exposure / LPR inflammation

Los síntomas son consistentes con RLF y no con ERGE clásico: sin ardor significativo, pero con fuerte compromiso de garganta y vías respiratorias.

Contexto Clave — Conexión Tiroidea
Diagnóstico reciente de condición tiroidea autoinmune (anticuerpos TPO elevados). Actualmente en tratamiento con levotiroxina, aproximadamente 6–8 semanas de tratamiento. Es probablemente el factor principal de la escalada:
  • Motilidad gástrica reducida — los alimentos permanecen más tiempo, mayor oportunidad de reflujo
  • Motilidad esofágica reducida — el ácido se elimina más lentamente cuando hay reflujo
  • Reactividad histamínica elevada — las membranas mucosas reaccionan exageradamente a la exposición ácida
  • Arquitectura del sueño alterada — menor reparación tisular nocturna

La escalada de episodios ocasionales a episodios nocturnos se correlaciona con la línea temporal de la disfunción tiroidea (~2 años). Se espera mejoría a medida que los niveles tiroideos se optimicen.
